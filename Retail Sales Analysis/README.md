# 📊 Retail Sales Analysis Dashboard

An interactive Power BI dashboard developed to analyze retail business performance from both sales and quantity perspectives. The dashboard consists of two dedicated analysis pages—Sales Analysis and Quantity Analysis—providing insights into product performance, customer contribution, city-wise trends, and regional distribution.

## 📌 Project Overview

The Retail Sales Analysis Dashboard provides a comprehensive view of retail performance through separate sales and quantity-based analysis. It enables users to explore product performance, customer contribution, geographical trends, and overall business performance through interactive visualizations and dynamic filtering.

The dashboard is divided into two pages, allowing users to analyze the data from both revenue and product quantity perspectives.

---

## 📈 Sales Analysis

The Sales Analysis page focuses on overall revenue performance and helps identify the major contributors to total sales.

### Key Performance Indicators

- Total Sales
- Total Sales Records
- Total Stores

### Visualizations

- Product Name vs Sales
- Customer vs Sales
- Product vs Sales
- City vs Sales
- Region vs Sales
- Customer-wise Sales Summary

### Dynamic Insights

The Sales Analysis page includes a dynamic Insights panel that automatically updates based on the selected filters and visual interactions.

As users explore different regions or interact with dashboard visuals, the Insights panel dynamically highlights:

- Highest-performing product
- Top revenue-generating customer
- Highest-performing city
- Lowest-performing product

This provides context-specific business insights and helps users quickly understand performance changes across different selections.

---

## 📦 Quantity Analysis

The Quantity Analysis page focuses on product quantity distribution and helps analyze product demand across customers, cities, and regions.

### Key Performance Indicators

- Total Quantity
- Total Products
- Total Customers
- Total Unit Price

### Visualizations

- Product vs Quantity
- Customer vs Quantity
- Quantity by Customer Name
- City vs Quantity
- Region vs Quantity
- Top 5 Items by Sales

The quantity-based analysis provides a different perspective from the sales analysis by focusing on product movement and demand rather than revenue. It helps identify how product quantities are distributed across customers and geographical locations.
